Why it happens

If the brain is still holding onto stress, problem-solving, or strong stimulation, it stays closer to wakefulness and the shift into sleep takes longer.

What sleep science suggests

As sleep approaches, attention loosens, outside information matters less, and the brain starts moving into a quieter, less effortful state. That shift needs calm more than force.

What to do tonight

Help the transition by lowering light, lowering stimulation, and choosing one softer cue that gives the brain permission to stop working so hard.
