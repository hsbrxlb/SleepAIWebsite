Clear answers, softer pacing, and a practical next step.

Why it happens

Without a steady transition, each night starts from zero. The brain has to guess whether it is still in work mode, worry mode, or actual rest mode.

What sleep science suggests

Repetition helps the brain predict what comes next. When the same softer cues show up night after night, the mind and body start moving into sleep with less resistance.

What to do tonight

Keep the routine short and believable: lower light, stop planning, choose one calm practice, and repeat it often enough that bedtime stops feeling random.
