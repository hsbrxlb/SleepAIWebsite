Why it happens

Night reduces distraction, increases inward attention, and often leaves the mind alone with unfinished pressure. That is why anxiety can feel more intense after dark even when nothing new has happened.

What sleep science suggests

The body also notices tension differently at night. Faster heartbeat, shallow breathing, and mental rehearsal stand out more when the room is quiet and the goal is rest.

What to do tonight

Keep the night small. Lower input, avoid starting new problems, and use a calmer cue like a story, slower breathing, or guided support to help the nervous system de-escalate.
