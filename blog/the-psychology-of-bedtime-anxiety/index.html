What you will find here.

Clear answers, softer pacing, and a practical next step.

Why it happens

If the bed has become the place where you worry, fail to sleep, or monitor yourself, the mind starts anticipating that pattern before the night even begins.

What sleep science suggests

This is a form of learned arousal. The brain starts pairing bedtime with effort, frustration, and performance pressure instead of safety and rest.

What to do tonight

Reduce the pressure in the routine, stop treating the bed like a test, and build a gentler pre-sleep sequence that changes the emotional tone before you lie down.
