Why it happens

At night there are fewer distractions, which gives unresolved tasks, emotions, and worries more space to repeat. That is why the mind can suddenly feel busiest when everything else is finally quiet.

What sleep science suggests

This pattern is often linked to hyperarousal. The brain stays in a problem-solving or threat-monitoring mode even though the body has already reached bedtime.

What to do tonight

Externalize what is circling, lower light and stimulation, and move attention into one soft anchor like breath, story, or guided audio instead of trying to force the thoughts away.
