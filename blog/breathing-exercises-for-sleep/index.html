Keep the exercise simple

Bedtime breathwork should feel slower and easier, not like a performance. If the pattern is too complex, it can keep the mind activated instead of settling.

Three patterns that help

Try lengthening the exhale, counting a steady in-and-out rhythm, or pairing breath with body weight sinking into the bed. The best pattern is the one that feels calm enough to keep following.

When breathwork starts feeling effortful

If you begin monitoring the breath too closely, switch into softer guidance, story, or ambient sound. The goal is less inner effort, not more.

Pufflo can help the night move from active control into calmer support, which is why breathing often works best inside a broader bedtime flow. From here, continue to Guided sleep meditation or AI sleep stories.
