Why it happens

When people are tense or mentally busy, the breath often stays short and shallow. That keeps the body closer to day mode and makes sleep feel farther away.

What sleep science suggests

Slower breathing, especially a longer exhale, signals more safety to the nervous system. That makes it easier for heart rate, muscle tension, and alertness to ease down together.

What to do tonight

Keep the pattern simple. Try lengthening the exhale, counting a steady in-and-out rhythm, or pairing breath with body weight sinking into the bed. If breathing starts feeling effortful, switch into softer guidance or story.
