Why it happens

Once the day quiets down, there is less distraction and more space for the nervous system to notice tension, uncertainty, and unfinished emotion.

What sleep science suggests

An anxious night often combines lower external input with a body that is still carrying stress chemistry and higher alertness. That is why a normal worry can feel amplified at bedtime.

What to do tonight

Lower stimulation, reduce light, stop problem-solving, and use slower breathing or gentler audio so the body gets more cues of safety before sleep.
