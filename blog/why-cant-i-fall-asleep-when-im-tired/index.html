Why it happens

Many people reach bed physically drained but still mentally active. The body is tired, but the nervous system is still scanning, looping, or holding onto the day.

What sleep science suggests

Sleep arrives more easily when alertness falls. Stress, unresolved emotion, bright screens, and pressure to sleep can all keep the brain too switched on even when the body wants rest.

What to do tonight

Reduce stimulation, stop problem-solving, and move attention into something softer such as slower breathing, guided support, or a calmer story rather than forcing sleep.
