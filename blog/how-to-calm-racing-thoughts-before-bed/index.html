Guide

What you will find here.

Clear answers, softer pacing, and a practical next step.

Why it happens

Thought loops often show up when worries feel unfinished. The mind keeps rehearsing, reviewing, or predicting because it has not yet felt safe enough to stop.

What sleep science suggests

When attention keeps snapping back to the same thought, the brain stays more alert. The goal is not to win an argument with the thought. It is to lower the need to keep following it.

What to do tonight

Name the thought, write down what matters, then move attention into one steady cue like breath, voice, or story. Keep the cue simple enough that it does not create a new task.
