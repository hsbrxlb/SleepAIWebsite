Why it happens

Many people go straight from work, messages, or problem-solving into bed. That abrupt shift leaves the brain carrying daytime speed into a moment that needs the opposite.

What sleep science suggests

Sleep comes more easily when the body and mind get a smoother transition. Lower light, lower input, and repetition all help the brain recognize that wakeful effort is ending.

What to do tonight

Use the last 20 minutes to dim lights, put away demanding screens, stop planning tomorrow, and move into one calming activity like a story, slower breathing, or guided support.
